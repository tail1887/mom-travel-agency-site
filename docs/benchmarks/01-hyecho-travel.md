# 01. Hyecho Travel Benchmark

Source: https://www.hyecho.com/

## Positioning

Hyecho Travel presents itself as a long-running specialty travel agency focused on trekking, cultural exploration, and special destinations. Search results describe categories such as Nepal/Himalaya, China, Japan, India, Tibet, the Middle East, and Europe.

## Useful Patterns

### Specialty First

- The site is not a generic travel mall.
- It communicates domain expertise through destination and theme categories.
- This is useful if the new agency chooses a specialty such as pilgrimage, India, senior long-haul travel, or small-group cultural travel.

### Product Detail Depth

Observed product detail examples include:

- Theme
- Difficulty level
- Accommodation type
- Highest altitude
- Walking distance
- Travel dates
- Duration
- Remaining/available reservation status
- Minimum departure count
- Price
- Product manager contact information

This is useful because specialty travel products need more than a title and price. The product page should prove that the agency understands operational details.

### Trust Through Specificity

- Manager contact information and detailed product metadata support confidence.
- Difficulty, lodging, distance, and minimum departure count reduce ambiguity.

## Design Implications

- Use structured product facts near the top of detail pages.
- Use badges for theme, difficulty, departure status, and duration.
- Avoid hiding operational details deep in long text.
- Give the product manager or consultation path a visible area.

## Operator Implications

Admin product fields may need:

- Theme
- Difficulty or trip intensity
- Accommodation
- Duration
- Departure dates
- Minimum departure count
- Current booking status
- Product manager
- Contact phone/email

## Patterns To Avoid

- Do not overload the first screen with too many technical details.
- Do not require the MVP operator to manage highly specialized fields unless the agency truly needs them.

## Prototype Usage

Best suited for:

- Traditional trust prototype
- Product marketplace prototype
- Specialty travel product detail prototype
