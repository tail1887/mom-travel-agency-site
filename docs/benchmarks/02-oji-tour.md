# 02. Oji Tour Benchmark

Source: https://www.ojitour.com/

## Positioning

Oji Tour is positioned around South America, Central America, Africa, and special-region travel. Public search snippets emphasize long-running expertise, South America group departures, and specialty destination operations.

## Useful Patterns

### Expertise Led Trust

- The site and related channel descriptions emphasize years of experience and accumulated regional know-how.
- This is useful for destinations where customers worry about safety, logistics, language, and local reliability.

### Specialty Region Focus

- Strong regional focus makes the agency easier to remember.
- It avoids looking like a generic price-comparison site.

### Pre-Consultation Confidence

Oji Tour-related public content emphasizes:

- Professional tour leaders
- Transparent inclusion/exclusion choices
- Carefully planned accommodations, transport, and tours
- Pilot products adjusted from customer feedback

These patterns are useful for a site that wants customers to consult before paying.

## Design Implications

- Place expertise proof near the hero or first product section.
- Use short evidence blocks such as years of experience, specialty regions, group operation count, or guide expertise.
- Make consultation feel like a professional travel planning step, not a generic contact form.

## Operator Implications

Admin-managed content may include:

- Specialty region claims
- Guide or tour leader notes
- Departure status
- Product explanation sessions
- Included/excluded item transparency

## Patterns To Avoid

- Do not make experience claims unless they are true and verifiable.
- Do not make the page feel too text-heavy before the user sees actual products.

## Prototype Usage

Best suited for:

- Consultation landing prototype
- Premium custom prototype
- Content/story prototype
