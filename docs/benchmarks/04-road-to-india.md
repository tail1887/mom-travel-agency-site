# 04. The Road To India Benchmark

Source: https://www.indoro.co.kr/

## Positioning

The Road To India is presented as a specialty travel agency for India and other special regions. Public search snippets mention India, Africa, Central/South America, Central Asia, the Middle East, the Mediterranean, Europe, Mongolia, Southeast Asia, China, and Tibet.

## Useful Patterns

### Named Specialty Identity

- The brand name itself suggests a strong destination story.
- This is useful when the new agency wants to be known for a specific region or travel philosophy.

### Special Region Confidence

- Special-region travel requires customers to trust planning, safety, and local knowledge.
- The site category direction suggests that product pages should show itinerary depth and operator expertise.

### External Channel Support

Public profiles and Kakao channel information expose:

- Representative phone number
- Office hours
- Physical address
- Specialty-region positioning

These trust and contact signals are important for consultation-heavy travel.

## Design Implications

- A strong identity can come from a destination phrase, not only a logo.
- Use destination maps, route language, and itinerary-led storytelling if the agency has a specialty.
- Keep contact information visible for customers who need reassurance.

## Operator Implications

Admin-managed content may include:

- Region categories
- Product route summaries
- Itinerary highlights
- Contact and office information
- Channel links

## Patterns To Avoid

- Do not claim special-region expertise without matching product detail.
- Do not make the site so niche that customers cannot find general inquiry paths.

## Prototype Usage

Best suited for:

- Premium custom prototype
- Content/story prototype
- Consultation landing prototype
